## Epic: kart-review-service v1

Review authorship (submission, edit, retraction), the two-stage hybrid moderation workflow (synchronous automated classifier + human queue), and the canonical rating aggregate (`ProductRating`, ADR-0014) Review — not Product — is the system-of-record for. Event-driven, not Saga-participating: Review reacts to Order's `OrderCreated`/`OrderDelivered` after the fact (ADR-0021, ADR-0005) and never calls Order synchronously (BRD §2.1 item 14; architecture.md's Boundary Rationale).

## Notes for Sprint Planner Agent

- REV-1 (classifier adapter) and REV-2 (`VerifiedPurchaseRecord` projection) are the two foundational tickets with no dependency on each other or on anything else — sequence both first, in parallel, mirroring `kart-payment-service/tickets.md`'s PAY-1/PAY-2 pairing.
- REV-3 (`SubmitReview`) is the root feature ticket: it is the first to create the `reviews`/`review_outbox` schema, and REV-4 through REV-11 all trace back to it, directly or transitively — the same "root every other slice needs" role `ORD-1` plays in `kart-order-service/tickets.md`.
- REV-4, REV-5, and REV-6 are three independent consumers of the same `review_outbox`/RabbitMQ mechanism REV-3 establishes (the relay poller, the Mongo read-model projector, and the internal `ProductRating` aggregate consumer) — they can be built in parallel once REV-3 lands, and share no code with each other (unlike `kart-payment-service/tickets.md`'s PAY-6/7/8 grouping, which does share one dedup mechanism — these three are independent readers of the same published-event stream, not variations of one handler).
- REV-9 (`EditReview`) and REV-11 (`ModerateReview`) are coupled through the `PendingRevision` staging mechanism REV-9 introduces — REV-11 must be built after REV-9 because two of its four outcome branches (accept/reject on a staged revision) only exist once `PendingRevision` is a real, populated field. REV-10 (`RetractReview`) has no such coupling to REV-9 and only depends on REV-3 — it can proceed in parallel with REV-9/REV-11 once REV-3 lands.
- REV-1 is shared infrastructure consumed by both REV-3 and REV-9 — recommend the engineer who builds REV-1 also validates its integration in both call sites, the same "shared-mechanism tickets" grouping recommendation `kart-offer-service/tickets.md` gives its own OFF-2/OFF-10 pair and `kart-payment-service/tickets.md` gives PAY-6/7/8.
- REV-7 (`GetProductRatingSummary`) and REV-8 (`ListReviewsBySku`) are the two pure-read tickets, each gated on its own respective projection (REV-6 and REV-5 respectively) being populated — no dependency between REV-7 and REV-8 themselves, can build in parallel.
- No circular dependencies in this graph. Longest chain is REV-1/REV-2 → REV-3 → REV-4 → REV-6 → REV-7 (5 deep), matched by REV-1/REV-2 → REV-3 → REV-9 → REV-11 (4 deep) — both reflect that every downstream slice needs `SubmitReview`'s schema and at least one of its own async consumers already in place before it has anything to read or react to.
- Out of scope for this service's own backlog: `kart-order-service/event-contract.md`'s `OrderCreated` consumer-list update (adding `kart-review-service`, per ADR-0021's Consequences section) is that service's own file, not a `kart-review-service` ticket — tracked there if/when that service's own pipeline revisits its `event-contract.md`. Likewise, `kart-product-service`'s and `kart-search-service`'s own future consumption of the new `ReviewUnpublished` event (`ddd-model.md`'s own flag) is a ticket for each of those services' own backlogs, not this one. The GDPR/`UserDataErased` erasure gap `database-design.md` flags (ADR-0016) is explicitly **not** ticketed here either — it requires a requirement-spec/ddd-model pass this service hasn't had yet, per that document's own text; inventing an erasure-handling ticket now would be building against a domain decision that doesn't exist yet.
